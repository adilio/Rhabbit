# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

A small invited circle — the maker, a partner, and a handful of allowlisted
friends and family. Access is granted by hand in the Firebase console, so the
user base never grows past people who were personally let in. Everyone has
their own private data; nobody sees anyone else's.

They open Rhabbit standing up: mid-morning with a coffee, last thing at night
in bed, one-handed on a phone. The job is to record that a thing happened and
put the phone away. Secondary sessions are reflective and rarer — reviewing a
month on History, reading what Progress noticed, adding a habit.

Every screen has one primary task. Today: log. History: fill a gap or check a
month. Progress: understand without being graded. Settings: adjust and leave.

## Product Purpose

A private habit tracker built around the daily act of logging rather than
around the data it accumulates. Boolean, numeric, duration, and avoidance
habits on flexible schedules; spreadsheet import so years of tracking
elsewhere carry over; calendar backfill, heatmaps, and plain-language
insights for the reflective sessions.

Success is that logging stays effortless and guilt-free: opening the app is a
two-second act, and a missed day never makes anyone want to quit or delete it.
A user who lapses for three weeks and comes back is the outcome the product
exists to produce.

## Positioning

The calmest possible daily ritual. Rhabbit is the two-second act of noting you
showed up — not a scoreboard, not a dashboard, not a coach.

## Brand Personality

Gentle, warm, playful. Storybook rather than clinical: Fraunces headings, a
pastel-blue rabbit, time-of-day glyphs, a hop path instead of a progress ring.
The voice is a friend who is genuinely pleased for you and slightly amused by
the whole enterprise — "One hop at a time," "the burrow is cozy tonight,"
"Skipped — no guilt, it's not a miss."

Whimsy lives in moments, not in the way. Character shows up in confirmations,
empty states, and the mark; it never costs a tap or slows a log. The app is
allowed to be a little silly; it is never allowed to be cute at the expense of
being fast.

## Anti-references

**Streak-guilt habit apps.** No flames, no chain-breaking, no red loss
warnings, no counter that resets to zero and means it. Streaks as a pressure
device are the thing Rhabbit is arguing against — skips, pauses, and comebacks
are first-class states, not excuses the app tolerates.

**Generic SaaS product UI.** No cream-and-slate neutrals, no identical card
grids, no tiny tracked uppercase eyebrows over every section, no hero-metric
template. Rhabbit already has a committed pastel identity; work should deepen
it, not regress toward the default look of an AI-generated app.

## Design Principles

**Forgiveness is structural.** Skipped, paused, and comeback are real states in
the data model and in the UI, not softened copy layered over a miss. If a
design decision can shame someone for a gap, it is the wrong decision.

**The log outranks everything.** The primary action on any screen is reachable
in one tap with a thumb, and every destructive or state-changing action has
undo. Nothing decorative may sit between a user and marking a habit done.

**Warmth earns its keep.** Character is welcome where it costs nothing —
confirmations, empty states, the mark, the hop path. It is not welcome in
labels, buttons, data, or anywhere it adds a beat to the task.

**Never a number that judges.** Counts describe; they don't grade. Progress
speaks in plain language about what happened, not in scores, grades, or
percentages framed as failure.

**Private means private.** No social layer, no comparison, no leaderboard, no
growth loop, no notification that exists to pull someone back. The only reason
to open Rhabbit is that they wanted to.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.2 AA as a hard floor across every surface: 4.5:1 for body text and
placeholders, 3:1 for large text and UI boundaries, visible focus on every
interactive element, and touch targets that hold up one-handed on a phone.
Motion respects `prefers-reduced-motion` with a crossfade or instant
alternative — the hop path and toast animations included. Both light and dark
themes are held to the same bar; neither is the afterthought.
